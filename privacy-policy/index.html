Introduction

Welcome to AniLog. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you visit our blog. We are committed to protecting your privacy and being transparent about our data practices.

Information We Collect

Analytics Data

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors interact with our blog. Google Analytics collects:

  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Pages visited and time spent on pages
  • Referring website
  • Geographic location (country/city level)
  • IP address (anonymized)
  • Device information

Cookies

We use cookies solely for analytics purposes through Google Analytics. These cookies help us understand:

  • How many people visit our blog
  • Which pages are most popular
  • How long visitors stay on our site
  • General traffic patterns

You can disable cookies in your browser settings at any time.

What We Don’t Collect

We want to be clear about what we do NOT collect:

  • We do not collect personal identifying information (name, email, phone number)
  • We do not require registration or user accounts
  • We do not collect payment information
  • We do not use tracking pixels or social media tracking
  • We do not sell or share your data with third parties (except Google Analytics as described)

How We Use Your Information

The analytics data we collect is used solely to:

  • Understand blog traffic and readership patterns
  • Improve content based on reader interests
  • Optimize site performance and user experience

Third-Party Services

Google Analytics

We use Google Analytics, a web analytics service provided by Google LLC. Google Analytics uses cookies to help us analyze how users interact with our blog.

Google’s use of analytics cookies is subject to their own Privacy Policy. You can learn more about how Google uses data at: https://policies.google.com/privacy

To opt out of Google Analytics tracking, you can:

  • Use browser extensions like Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on
  • Enable “Do Not Track” in your browser settings
  • Block cookies in your browser settings

Your Rights (GDPR Compliance)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have certain rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

  • Right to Access: You can request information about the data we process
  • Right to Rectification: You can request correction of inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure: You can request deletion of your data
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You can request limitation of data processing
  • Right to Data Portability: You can request transfer of your data
  • Right to Object: You can object to data processing
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: You can withdraw consent at any time

Since we only collect anonymous analytics data through Google Analytics, we do not store personal data that can be linked to you individually. However, you can exercise your rights by:

  • Disabling cookies in your browser
  • Using Google Analytics opt-out tools
  • Contacting us with any concerns

International Data Transfers

Google Analytics may transfer and process data in countries outside your own. Google complies with applicable data protection laws and frameworks for international data transfers.

Data Retention

Google Analytics retains data according to their retention policies. We have configured our Google Analytics to retain data for 26 months, after which it is automatically deleted.

Children’s Privacy

This blog is not directed at children under the age of 13 (or 16 in the EEA). We do not knowingly collect information from children.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The “Last Updated” date at the top of this page indicates when the policy was last revised. We encourage you to review this page periodically.

Your Consent

By using our blog, you consent to this Privacy Policy and our use of Google Analytics as described.

Cookie Notice

Additional Information for Specific Regions

California Residents (CCPA)

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), California residents have the right to know what personal information is collected and how it is used. As stated above, we only collect anonymous analytics data through Google Analytics.

EU/EEA Residents (GDPR)

Our lawful basis for processing analytics data is “legitimate interest” - understanding how our blog is used to improve content and user experience. You can object to this processing by disabling cookies or using opt-out mechanisms mentioned above.
